1. Trust Name

The name of the trust is: THE BONTHUYS FAMILY TRUST ("the Trust").

2. Definitions and Interpretation

"Beneficiaries" means the Primary and Secondary Beneficiaries described in clause 6.

"Capital" means all assets of the Trust, whether movable or immovable, corporeal or incorporeal, including accumulated income retained as capital.

"Connected Person" has the meaning assigned in section 1 of the Income Tax Act 58 of 1962.

"Founder" means Maria Jacoba Bonthuys (Identity Number: __________________________________).

"Income Tax Act" means the Income Tax Act 58 of 1962, as amended.

"Independent Trustee" means a Trustee who is not related to the Founder or any Beneficiary by blood or marriage, and who has no direct or indirect financial interest in the Trust other than trustee remuneration.

"Investment Property" means immovable residential or commercial property acquired by the Trust for the purpose of earning rental income and long-term capital growth, and which is not trading stock.

"Letters of Authority" means written authority issued by the Master of the High Court in terms of section 6 of the Act.

"Master" means the Master of the High Court with jurisdiction over the district in which the Trust is registered.

"Official Rate" means the official interest rate determined under section 7C of the Income Tax Act from time to time.

"SA Tax Year" means the period from 1 March to the last day of February of the following year, as defined under the Income Tax Act.

"Trustees" means the trustees appointed from time to time, acting jointly as required by this Deed.

"Trust Property" means all property, income, rights, proceeds, investments, and assets of the Trust.

2.2 Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation. Words in the singular include the plural and vice versa. A reference to a statute includes all amendments thereto.

3. Establishment of the Trust

3.1 The Founder hereby establishes an inter vivos irrevocable discretionary trust in terms of this Deed.

3.2 The Trust shall come into legal existence upon the issue of Letters of Authority by the Master.

3.3 The Trust is irrevocable save to the extent expressly permitted by this Deed and applicable law.

3.4 The Founder hereby irrevocably donates the sum of R100 (one hundred rand) to the Trust as initial Trust Property, receipt of which is hereby acknowledged.

3.5 No asset of the Trust shall form part of the personal estate of any Trustee or of the Founder, whether by operation of law or otherwise.

4. Purpose of the Trust

4.1 The Trust is established for the following primary purposes:

(a) To acquire, hold, manage, let, mortgage, improve, develop, and dispose of immovable Investment Property for the long-term benefit of Beneficiaries;

(b) To hold shares in and receive dividends from connected operating companies, including but not limited to tenbucks-mobile (Pty) Ltd and any successor or related entity;

(c) To invest in movable and incorporeal assets, including listed and unlisted securities, locally and offshore;

(d) To receive, hold, and administer insurance proceeds in terms of policies ceded to or owned by the Trust;

(e) To preserve and grow wealth for the benefit of Beneficiaries across generations;

(f) To provide structured succession planning ensuring the seamless transfer of family wealth without the delays, costs, and public exposure of estate administration;

(g) To protect Trust Property from the personal creditors of individual Trustees and Beneficiaries.

4.2 The Trust shall at all times be administered in compliance with South African law, including the Act, the Income Tax Act, and the Financial Intelligence Centre Act. The Trust shall not be used to evade creditors or taxation.

4.3 The Founder shall have no powers of control, direction, or instruction over the Trustees or the administration of Trust Property once Letters of Authority are issued.

4.4 The Trust acts as a permanent holding structure for capital assets and as a conduit for income distributed to Beneficiaries. The Trust is not intended to accumulate income — Trustees are directed to distribute income efficiently in accordance with clause 12.

5. Non-Domination and Sham Trust Safeguards

5.1 All Trustees shall exercise their powers independently, jointly, and in good faith for the benefit of Beneficiaries.

5.2 No Trustee, including the Founder, may dominate, direct, or unduly influence the decision-making of the other Trustees. Any instruction or request by the Founder or a Beneficiary to Trustees shall be treated as non-binding.

5.3 Trust Property shall at all times be administered separately from the personal estates of the Trustees, the Founder, and the Beneficiaries. Separate bank accounts, accounting records, and financial statements shall be maintained in the name of the Trust.

5.4 No Trustee shall treat Trust Property as personal property or use Trust Property for personal purposes except as a duly resolved Trustee distribution or remuneration.

5.5 All significant Trust decisions shall be evidenced by written Trustee resolutions, signed by the requisite quorum, and filed in the Trust's minute book.

5.6 The Independent Trustee shall maintain genuine independence. Any arrangement which would compromise the Independent Trustee's independence shall be disclosed to the Master and the affected Trustee shall recuse themselves from the relevant decision.

Degrees of consanguinity are calculated in terms of the Intestate Succession Act 81 of 1987. "Per stirpes" means that the share of a deceased person passes to their descendants in equal shares.

6.3 No Beneficiary shall have any vested right to income or capital of the Trust unless and until a Trustee resolution formally distributing a specified amount to that Beneficiary has been passed and the Beneficiary has been notified in writing.

6.4 No Beneficiary may cede, assign, pledge, or encumber any interest, actual or contingent, in the Trust or Trust Property. Any purported cession, assignment, or encumbrance shall be void.

6.5 A Beneficiary who is a Trustee may participate in resolutions relating to distributions to other Beneficiaries but shall recuse themselves from any resolution relating to a distribution to themselves.

6.6 The Trustees may add further Beneficiaries by unanimous resolution, subject to the Independent Trustee's consent and, where required, Master approval. No person may be added as a Beneficiary if doing so would constitute a sham or result in tax avoidance contrary to law.

7.2 Minimum Number and Independence Requirements

(a) The Trust shall at all times have a minimum of two Trustees.

(b) At least one Trustee must at all times be an Independent Trustee.

(c) If at any time there is no Independent Trustee, the remaining Trustees shall suspend all decisions requiring Independent Trustee consent (clause 8.2) until a new Independent Trustee is appointed.

7.3 Quorum

(a) A quorum shall consist of any two Trustees, one of whom must be the Independent Trustee.

(b) Decisions at meetings where a quorum is present shall be by majority vote, save for matters listed in clause 8.2 which require Independent Trustee consent.

(c) Trustees may act by written resolution without a formal meeting, provided all Trustees sign or provide written concurrence.

7.4 Disqualification

No person may act as Trustee if they are: sequestrated and unrehabilitaged; of unsound mind; a minor; removed by a court; or otherwise disqualified under the Act.

7.5 Remuneration

The Independent Trustee shall be entitled to reasonable remuneration as agreed by the Trustees from time to time and charged to the Trust. Family Trustees shall serve without remuneration unless the Trustees unanimously resolve otherwise.

8. Powers of Trustees

8.1 The Trustees shall have all powers necessary or expedient to achieve the Trust's purposes, including:

(a) To acquire, let, improve, encumber, and dispose of movable and immovable property;

(b) To hold shares in and exercise all rights attached to shares in any company, including tenbucks-mobile (Pty) Ltd;

(c) To borrow money on such terms as they deem fit, and to mortgage, pledge, or encumber Trust Property as security;

(d) To open and operate bank accounts in the name of the Trust;

(e) To invest in any lawful investment, whether local or foreign;

(f) To enter into agreements, contracts, and leases;

(g) To institute or defend legal proceedings;

(h) To appoint agents, attorneys, accountants, property managers, and other advisors;

(i) To receive management fees, rental income, dividends, interest, and other income on behalf of the Trust;

(j) To make distributions to Beneficiaries of income and/or capital by resolution.

8.2 Decisions Requiring Independent Trustee Consent

The following decisions require the specific written consent of the Independent Trustee and shall not be valid without it:

(a) Acquisition or disposal of immovable property or any asset with a value exceeding R500,000;

(b) Loans to or from Connected Persons (including any person related to a Trustee or Beneficiary);

(c) Capital distributions to any Beneficiary exceeding R500,000 in any SA Tax Year;

(d) Use of Trust assets as security for any obligation of a Trustee or Beneficiary personally;

(e) Amendment of this Trust Deed;

(f) Addition of new Beneficiaries;

(g) Termination of the Trust;

(h) Any transaction between the Trust and a Trustee or Connected Person on non-arm's-length terms.

8.3 Trustees shall exercise the care, diligence, and skill reasonably expected of a person managing the affairs of another, as required by section 9 of the Act.

8.4 Trustees shall keep minutes of all meetings and retain all resolutions in a minute book which shall be maintained at the Trust's registered address or such other address as notified to the Master.

9. Investments and Property Acquisition

9.1 Trustees may invest Trust Property in any lawful investment, including listed shares, bonds, unit trusts, local and foreign currencies, and offshore investments.

9.2 Residential Investment Property

The Trust may acquire residential Investment Property directly in the name of the Trust for the purpose of earning rental income and long-term capital appreciation. The following principles apply:

(a) Investment Property shall be recorded as a capital asset of the Trust and shall not be classified as trading stock;

(b) Rental income received in respect of Investment Property shall be distributed to Beneficiaries in terms of clause 12;

(c) The Trust may incur mortgage bond obligations to finance Investment Property acquisitions. Trustees may arrange personal suretyships from Trustees in their personal capacities to facilitate bond approvals;

(d) All proceeds on disposal of Investment Property shall be distributed or retained as capital in the Trust at the discretion of the Trustees;

(e) Investment Property shall at all times be held separately from any property held as trading stock in any connected operating entity.

9.3 Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency investments are permitted subject to the following conditions:

(a) Prior written consent of the Independent Trustee;

(b) A maximum allocation of 10% of the total market value of Trust assets at the time of investment;

(c) A documented risk acknowledgement resolution signed by all Trustees.

9.4 Trustees may hold shares in any company through which the Trust conducts business or investment activities, provided such holdings are consistent with the purposes of the Trust.

10. Insurance Proceeds

10.1 The Trust may be nominated as the beneficiary of, or may own, insurance policies on the lives of Trustees, Beneficiaries, or other persons connected to the Trust.

10.2 Where the Trust is to own a policy (rather than merely be nominated as a beneficiary), the Trust shall pay premiums from Trust funds. The distinction between Trust ownership and mere nomination determines whether proceeds fall inside or outside the insured's estate for estate duty purposes under section 3(3)(a) of the Estate Duty Act 45 of 1955.

10.3 Insurance proceeds received by the Trust shall be administered as Trust Property and distributed or retained in accordance with a Trustee resolution.

10.4 Trustees shall obtain professional tax advice prior to distributing insurance proceeds and shall have regard to applicable income tax, estate duty, and donations tax implications.

11. Funding, Donations and Loans

11.1 The Trust may be funded by donations, capital contributions, loans, income retained as capital, insurance proceeds, or any other lawful means.

11.2 Donations

Any person may donate funds or assets to the Trust. Each donor should have regard to the R100,000 annual donations tax exemption per donor under section 54 of the Income Tax Act. Donations within the annual exemption are free of donations tax.

11.3 Loans from Connected Persons

Any loan advanced to the Trust by a natural person, or by a company where that person is a Connected Person in relation to the Trust, shall bear interest at not less than the Official Rate as contemplated in section 7C of the Income Tax Act. Failure to charge the Official Rate will result in the interest shortfall being treated as a deemed donation, subject to donations tax on amounts exceeding the annual exemption.

11.4 All loans to and from the Trust shall be reduced to writing, signed by the parties, and retained in the Trust's records. The Independent Trustee must consent to any loan exceeding R100,000 in terms of clause 8.2.

11.5 All Trust transactions, including donations, loans, and distributions, shall be properly recorded in the Trust's accounting records.

12. Income Distribution and Tax Efficiency

12.1 The Trust is a discretionary trust. No Beneficiary has a fixed entitlement to any income or capital. All distributions are subject to a Trustee resolution passed in accordance with this Deed.

12.2 Annual Distribution Objective

The Trustees are directed to give serious consideration, at or before the end of each SA Tax Year (28 February), to distributing accumulated income of the Trust to Beneficiaries. Undistributed income of the Trust is taxed at 45% in the Trust's hands under the Income Tax Act. Distribution of income to Beneficiaries allows taxation at the individual Beneficiary's marginal rate, which may be substantially lower.

12.3 Income Character

In terms of section 25B of the Income Tax Act, income distributed to Beneficiaries retains the character it had in the Trust (rental income, dividend income, interest income, capital gain, etc.). Trustees shall record the character of each distribution in the relevant resolution.

12.4 Minor Beneficiaries

Where a Beneficiary is a minor and their parent has funded the Trust by way of loan or donation, section 7(3) of the Income Tax Act may attribute income distributed to the minor back to the parent for tax purposes. Trustees shall take specialist tax advice on distributions to minor Beneficiaries and shall preference distributions to adult Beneficiaries where this results in a lower overall tax burden.

12.5 Capital Distributions

Capital distributions (including capital gains realised on disposal of Trust assets) shall be made by Trustee resolution. Capital gain distributions to individual Beneficiaries are subject to capital gains tax at a 40% inclusion rate at the Beneficiary's marginal rate, which is more favourable than the 80% inclusion rate applicable to the Trust or any company holding those assets.

12.6 Management Fee Income

Where the Trust receives income in the form of management or administrative fees from connected operating companies, such income shall be treated as ordinary income of the Trust and distributed to Beneficiaries in accordance with this clause. The Trust shall maintain proper invoices and service agreements to support such fee arrangements at arm's length.

13. Taxation

13.1 The Trustees shall ensure compliance with all applicable tax obligations of the Trust, including registration with the South African Revenue Service (SARS) as a taxpayer (IT12T), submission of annual income tax returns, and payment of any tax due.

13.2 The Trustees may distribute income or capital in a tax-efficient manner where this is lawful and consistent with the purposes of the Trust and the Act.

13.3 Trustees shall obtain professional tax advice in respect of: section 7C deemed donations; section 7(3) attribution of minor beneficiary income; section 25B income character passthrough; and capital gains tax on disposals of Trust assets.

13.4 The Trust shall be registered with the South African Revenue Service within 30 days of receiving Letters of Authority from the Master.

14. Accounting and Records

14.1 Proper accounting records shall be maintained in respect of all Trust Property, income, expenditure, loans, and distributions. Records shall be maintained separately from the personal accounts of any Trustee.

14.2 Annual financial statements shall be prepared in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles (IFRS for SMEs or equivalent) within 6 months of the end of each SA Tax Year.

14.3 An independent review shall be conducted where required by the Master, required by law, or where the Trustees deem it prudent.

14.4 The Beneficial Ownership Register of the Trust shall be maintained and updated with the Companies and Intellectual Property Commission (CIPC) as required by the General Laws (Anti-Money Laundering and Combating Terrorism Financing) Amendment Act 22 of 2022 and the Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001.

15. Minor Beneficiaries

15.1 Where a Beneficiary is a minor, the Trustees may apply income or capital of the Trust for the maintenance, education, healthcare, and general welfare of that Beneficiary.

15.2 Amounts set aside for a minor Beneficiary may be retained in the Trust until the Trustees determine it appropriate to distribute, which may be beyond the date the Beneficiary reaches majority, at the Trustees' absolute discretion.

15.3 Trustees shall have regard to section 7(3) of the Income Tax Act when considering distributions to minor Beneficiaries and shall document their tax analysis in the relevant resolution.

16. Resignation, Death and Continuity

16.1 A Trustee shall vacate office upon: death; permanent incapacity; sequestration; disqualification by the Master; written resignation with 30 days' notice to the remaining Trustees; or removal by court order.

16.2 The remaining Trustees shall appoint a replacement Trustee within 60 days of a vacancy arising. If the vacancy leaves the Trust without an Independent Trustee, no decisions in terms of clause 8.2 may be taken until the vacancy is filled.

16.3 The Trust shall not terminate upon the death, resignation, or incapacity of any Trustee. Trust Property shall vest in the remaining Trustees pending appointment of a replacement.

16.4 Any Trustee appointed in terms of this clause shall sign an Acceptance of Trusteeship and the appointment shall be registered with the Master.

17. Amendment

17.1 This Deed may be amended only by unanimous written resolution of all Trustees, including the Independent Trustee.

17.2 Amendments shall be submitted to the Master for approval where required under the Act.

17.3 No amendment may alter the irrevocable nature of the Trust or remove the requirement for an Independent Trustee.

18. Termination

18.1 The Trust may terminate only upon:

(a) The fulfilment of its stated purposes and the exhaustion of all Trust Property;

(b) A unanimous Trustee resolution to terminate, subject to Master approval; or

(c) A court order.

18.2 Upon termination, the Trustees shall distribute the remaining Trust Property among the then-living Beneficiaries, having regard to:

(a) The financial dependency of each Beneficiary;

(b) Equality among Beneficiaries of the same class;

(c) Benefits previously received by each Beneficiary;

(d) The long-term financial welfare and age of each Beneficiary.

18.3 All tax obligations of the Trust shall be settled before final distribution of Trust Property.

19. Governing Law

This Trust Deed shall be governed, construed, and interpreted in accordance with the laws of the Republic of South Africa. Any dispute arising from this Deed shall be subject to the jurisdiction of the High Court of South Africa.

Annexure A — Stress-Test Analysis
Litigation, SARS, Divorce & Insolvency Protections Built into this Deed

A1. Sham Trust & Control Attack (Parker / Badenhorst line of cases)

Risk: Court or SARS alleges the Trust is the alter ego of the Founder or a Trustee.

Protections: Irrevocable structure; Independent Trustee mandatory and has veto on high-risk decisions; express non-domination clause (clause 5); Trustee decision minutes required; Founder excluded from control post-Letters of Authority; Trust assets separately banked and accounted.

Practical requirement: Maria Bonthuys (as Founder-Trustee) must not unilaterally direct Trust decisions. The Independent Trustee must genuinely sign all clause 8.2 decisions. If Maria's practical conduct mirrors sole control, the sham trust risk increases regardless of the deed.

High resistance — subject to conduct consistency

A2. Divorce Attack

Risk: A Trustee's or Beneficiary's spouse attempts to include Trust assets in a matrimonial property claim.

Protections: No vested beneficiary rights (clause 6.3); Trustee discretion clearly framed; Founder excluded from post-registration control; Trust assets segregated; Independent Trustee governance; no asset encumbrance by Beneficiaries (clause 6.4).

Strong defence against accrual or redistribution claims

A3. Insolvency & Creditor Attack

Risk: Personal creditors of a Trustee or Beneficiary attempt to attach Trust assets.

Protections: Anti-alienation (clause 6.4); no Beneficiary vesting (clause 6.3); discretionary distributions; Trust not used to evade creditors (clause 4.2). Assets transferred to the Trust more than 6 months before insolvency are generally protected.

Note: Transfers to the Trust within 6 months before a sequestration application may be challenged under the Insolvency Act.

High protection — subject to timing and conduct

A4. SARS — Section 7C (Interest-Free Loans)

Risk: SARS levies donations tax on interest shortfall on loans to the Trust or connected entities.

Protections: Clause 11.3 mandates Official Rate interest on all Connected Person loans; written loan agreements required; Independent Trustee oversight; explicit tax compliance obligations. Annual R100,000 donations tax exemption applies per donor.

Operational note: Donors who prefer to use the R100,000 annual exemption as outright donations (rather than loans) avoid section 7C entirely on those amounts. Loans above the annual exemption must bear Official Rate interest or the shortfall becomes a deemed donation attracting 20% donations tax.

SARS-compliant if correctly implemented

A5. Section 7(3) — Minor Beneficiary Income Attribution

Risk: Income distributed to minor Beneficiaries (Shone Riani Bonthuys while under 18) attributed to the parent (Marius Bonthuys) and taxed at Marius's marginal rate.

Protections: Clause 12.4 directs Trustees to take specialist advice and preference adult Beneficiary distributions. Income not sourced from Marius's contributions is lower risk for attribution. Prioritise distributions to Dajahn Nume Bonthuys (adult) until Shone reaches majority.

Manageable — with careful annual distribution planning
Annexure B — Will & Estate Planning Alignment

B1. Core Principles

The Wills of both Maria Jacoba Bonthuys (Founder) and Marius Bonthuys (Trustee) must be aligned with this Trust Deed. The Trust is a separate legal estate — Trust assets do not form part of the deceased's personal estate and must not be dealt with in any Will as if they do.

B2. Loan Accounts

Any loan account owed by the Trust to a deceased Trustee or donor forms part of that person's personal estate and must be dealt with expressly in their Will. The executor may claim the loan, write it off, or bequeath it to the Trust. Failure to address this can result in the loan amount being included in the deceased estate for estate duty purposes.

B3. Life Policies

Where the Trust owns a life policy and pays the premiums, the proceeds fall outside the estate of the insured. Where the insured pays premiums personally, proceeds may fall inside the estate under section 3(3)(a) of the Estate Duty Act. Premium payer should be the Trust to the maximum extent possible.

B4. Marius Bonthuys — specific note

Marius holds a life policy of R10,000,000. Action required: cede this policy to the Trust after Letters of Authority are received. The Trust must then pay premiums from Trust funds. This removes R10,000,000 from Marius's personal estate, saving up to R2,000,000 in estate duty.
