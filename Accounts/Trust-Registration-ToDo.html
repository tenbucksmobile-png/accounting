Bonthuys Family Trust
Registration & Implementation Plan — Internal Use Only

To-Do List

Priority order — June 2026 onwards
How this works: The trust is a two-mode structure. Mode 1 — it holds capital assets permanently on its balance sheet (no tax while held). Mode 2 — it passes income through to beneficiaries before 28 February each year to avoid the 45% undistributed income rate. Each step below either moves an asset onto the trust's balance sheet, or puts the cash-flow infrastructure in place so income can flow through correctly.

2
Establish the Trust's Administrative Infrastructure
Within 60 days of receiving Letters of Authority
Immediately After Registration
  • Open the trust bank account
    In the name of "The Bonthuys Family Trust." Bring: Letters of Authority (certified copy) + all three trustees' IDs + trust deed. Most major banks (FNB, Nedbank, ABSA, Standard Bank) have a trust account product. The account must be in the trust's name only — never combined with any personal or company account.
    ⚠ Nothing can flow into or out of the trust until this account exists. All subsequent steps depend on it.
  • Register the trust with SARS — obtain IT12T number
    Register at a SARS branch or via eFiling with the Letters of Authority and trust deed. The IT12T number is the trust's unique tax reference. Update the trust deed cover page and the accounting system once received.
  • File the Beneficial Ownership Register with CIPC
    Required under the General Laws (Anti-Money Laundering) Amendment Act 22 of 2022. Must be filed within 10 business days of trust registration and updated annually. Discloses the beneficial owners (Dajahn, Shone) and controlling persons (trustees).
  • Pass initial trustee resolutions (Annexure F of the trust deed)
    Resolutions required: acceptance of trusteeship · bank account mandate · SARS registration · appointment of accountants · adoption of SA tax year-end (28 Feb) · acceptance of annual donations from Maria and Marius · management fee arrangement with tenbucks-mobile. All three trustees must sign. Keep original resolutions in the trust minute book.

Cede the Life Policy to the Trust
Marius's R10m policy — moves R10m outside his estate. Saves R2m in estate duty.
Within 3 Months of Trust Bank Account Opening
  • Obtain the policy schedule from the insurer
    Confirm: policy number · insurer name · current sum assured (R10,000,000) · annual premium · current owner (Marius) · current beneficiary nomination. Update the trust's accounting system with these details.
  • Execute the cession of the policy to the trust
    Marius signs the insurer's cession forms, transferring ownership of the policy to "The Bonthuys Family Trust." The trust becomes the owner and the beneficiary. Requires: Letters of Authority (certified copy) + trustee resolution accepting the cession.
    ⚠ The cession must be absolute (full ownership transfer), not merely a nomination. A nomination keeps the policy in Marius's estate — only full ownership by the trust removes it.

    From the date of cession, premiums must be paid from the trust's bank account — not from Marius's personal account. If Marius continues paying personally, SARS may argue he retained a beneficial interest and include the policy in his estate. Fund the trust premium payments from the management fee income received from tenbucks-mobile.

    In the trust's life policy record: set trust_owns_policy = true, enter the policy number, insurer, and annual premium. Record the annual premium payment as an expense in the trust's accounts.
  • Update Marius's Will to reference the cession
    Marius's Will must include the specimen clause from Annexure G of the trust deed: recording that the policy has been ceded to the trust and that proceeds do not form part of his estate. Also include the trust acknowledgement clause and the loan account clause. Maria's Will must similarly exclude trust assets and deal with her loan account against the trust.

Ongoing Annual Obligations
Every year — non-negotiable compliance and tax discipline
Annual — Before 28 Feb
  • Trustee meeting — pass annual distribution resolution
    Before 28 February each year: all three trustees meet (or sign a written resolution) and formally distribute all trust income to beneficiaries. Specify amounts, beneficiary names, and income character (rental / management fee / capital gain). Tanja must sign. This is the single most important annual action — it prevents the 45% undistributed income tax.
  • Maria and Marius each donate R100k to the trust
    Transfer R100,000 each from personal accounts to the trust bank account before 28 Feb. Recorded as capital contributions (not income). Builds deposit reserve for the next apartment.
  • Maria forgives R100k of the loan balance as annual donation
    This is separate from the R100k capital donation above — Maria uses her annual exemption to write down the loan she is owed by the trust (from the land/share transfers). Note: the interest charged on the loan also counts against the R100k exemption. Signed addendum to the loan agreement + trustee resolution required.
    ⚠ Maria has one R100k annual exemption. Coordinate the loan write-down and any other donations she makes in the same tax year so the total does not exceed R100k without triggering 20% donations tax on the excess.
  • File the trust's IT12T tax return with SARS
    Annual income tax return for the trust. Discloses all income received, distributions made, and calculates tax (45% on any undistributed amount). If all income is distributed, tax = minimal. Deadline: confirmed by SARS each year (typically October–January for the prior tax year).
  • Update CIPC Beneficial Ownership Register annually
    The trust's BO register must be updated if any trustees, beneficiaries, or holdings change. Also update the BO register of tenbucks-mobile annually (now owned by the trust).
